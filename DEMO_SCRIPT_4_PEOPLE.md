## ü§î Q&A PREPARATION

### Likely Questions:

**Q: "How do you prevent defaults?"**
**A (ANALYST):** "Three mechanisms: (1) Savings streak requirement shows discipline. (2) Group stake - peers lose money if you default. (3) On-time repayments earn SAVE tokens and badges - positive reinforcement."

**Q: "What if everyone in a group defaults?"**
**A (ANALYST):** "Groups are capped at 8 people. Max loan is 250 LabUSDT. Each group can only have one active loan at a time. The treasury is overcollateralized. And we have a governance pause function for emergencies."

**Q: "Why would people use this vs a bank?"**
**A (NARRATOR):** "Most of our target users CAN'T use banks. They're unbanked. No credit history. No collateral. Banks won't serve them. We will. Plus, 2% vs 50% interest - no competition."

**Q: "How do you make money?"**
**A (CLOSER):** "The 2% interest goes to the treasury. SAVE token holders can vote to allocate funds - ecosystem growth, development, insurance pool. It's sustainable and community-controlled."

**Q: "What about regulation?"**
**A (CLOSER):** "We're a protocol, not a bank. Users interact with smart contracts directly. Like Uniswap or Aave. But we're targeting regions with favorable DeFi regulations. And we're working with local partners for compliance."

**Q: "How does it scale?"**
**A (ANALYST):** "Smart contracts scale infinitely. No loan officers needed. No branches. No paperwork. One deployment can serve millions. Gas costs are minimal on DIDLab."

**Q: "Who are your competitors?"**
**A (CLOSER):** "Traditional microfinance like Grameen Bank - but they're centralized and expensive. DeFi protocols like Aave - but they require crypto collateral. We're the first to combine microfinance principles with DeFi technology."
